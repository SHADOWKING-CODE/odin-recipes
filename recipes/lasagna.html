About lasagna

Lasagna is a traditional Italian dish that dates back to ancient times. It originated in

Italy, likely from the region of Emilia-Romagna. The word “lasagna” comes from the

Greek word laganon meaning flat sheet of pasta. Over time, it evolved into the

layered dish we know today, typically made with pasta, meat sauce, bechamel or

ricotta cheese, and mozzarella. It’s now enjoyed worldwide in many delicious

variations

{unordered list}

  • 12 lasagna noodles
  • 1 pound (450g) ground beef
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 24 oz (680g) marinara sauce
  • 15 oz (425g) ricotta cheese
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 egg
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh basil or parsley (optional, for garnish)

[order list]

*steps*

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Cook lasagna noodles according to package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  3. In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Sauté chopped onion and garlic until soft.
  4. Add ground beef to the skillet. Cook until browned, breaking it apart as it cooks.
  5. Stir in marinara sauce, season with salt and pepper, and let it simmer for 10–15 minutes.
  6. In a bowl, mix ricotta cheese, egg, and half of the Parmesan cheese.
  7. In a baking dish, spread a layer of meat sauce on the bottom.
  8. Add a layer of lasagna noodles, then spread ricotta mixture, followed by mozzarella cheese.
  9. Repeat layers (sauce, noodles, ricotta, mozzarella) until ingredients are used up, finishing with meat sauce and a topping of mozzarella and remaining Parmesan.
  10. Cover with foil and bake for 25 minutes.
  11. Remove foil and bake an additional 15 minutes until the cheese is bubbly and golden.
  12. Let rest for 10 minutes before serving. Garnish with chopped basil or parsley if desired.
