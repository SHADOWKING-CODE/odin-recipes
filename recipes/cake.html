Origin of Cake:

The origin of cake dates back to ancient times. The earliest cakes were more like bread, sweetened with honey, and often included nuts and dried fruits.

The word "cake" comes from the Old Norse word "kaka", introduced to English during Viking invasions.

In medieval Europe, cakes became more distinct from bread, especially when cooks started using butter, eggs, and sugar.

Modern cakes, as we know them—light and fluffy—emerged in the 18th century, especially with the invention of leavening agents like baking soda and baking powder.
